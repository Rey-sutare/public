About Us

The Olo-Olo Mangrove Forest and Eco Park is a sustainable eco-tourism destination located in the coastal barangay of Olo-Olo, Lobo, Batangas. It was established through the efforts of the Olo-Olo Seaside Workers Association (OSWA) in partnership with the local government to protect and conserve the mangrove ecosystem while promoting environmental education and livelihood for the community.

Whether you're seeking eco-education through guided tours, peaceful kayaking along mangrove-lined waterways, birdwatching of resident and migratory species, or simply enjoying the serene beauty of this coastal paradise, the OLO-OLO Eco Park offers an unforgettable immersion into nature's delicate balance.

Rules & Guidelines

Environmental Guidelines

  • Do not litter or throw trash into the sea or mangrove areas
  • Avoid cutting or damaging mangrove trees and plants
  • Keep noise levels low to avoid disturbing wildlife
  • Follow designated paths and boardwalks
  • Observe waste segregation and proper disposal
  • Follow instructions from guides and staff

House Rules

  • No unregistered guests allowed
  • No smoking inside the Pyramid Hut and Closed Kubo
  • Drink in moderation
  • Guests are prohibited from swimming after 8 PM
  • Dispose of garbage in trash cans
  • Keep a close eye on your kids
  • Report any damage in a timely manner
  • For any questions, kindly communicate with staff

Emergency Guidelines

  • In case of emergency, notify park personnel immediately
  • Lifeguards and trained responders are on standby during operational hours
  • The nearest medical facility is Lobo District Hospital, about 10–15 minutes away
  • Familiarize yourself with emergency exits and assembly points
  • In case of extreme weather or natural hazards, follow staff evacuation instructions

Your cooperation in following these guidelines helps us preserve the natural beauty of OLO-OLO Mangrove Forest and ensure the safety of all visitors.

BAYAN NG LOBO - I LOVE LOBO BATANGAS | ORDINANCE NO. 2015-04

What is Environmental Users' Fee (EUF)?

It refers to charges imposed on any person who visits or enters the Municipality of Lobo and is not a resident thereof, for the purpose of enjoying and experiencing the natural wonders of the various tourist destinations and the services provided by the natural environment or habitat within the Municipality of Lobo. This fee is collected to support environmental conservation and maintenance of the eco-park facilities.

Fee & Duration

₱20.00 per person

Valid for entire stay in Lobo until exit/departure

Collected at entry points by authorized collectors

Who's Exempted?

  • PWD (Persons With Disabilities)
  • Senior Citizens (20% discount)
